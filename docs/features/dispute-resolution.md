
# Dispute Resolution Flow

This document explains the process for resolving disputes related to bounty payments in SolFoundry.

## Appeal Process

When a contributor believes a bounty has been unfairly rejected, they can initiate an appeal following these steps:

### 1. Appeal Request
- The contributor submits an appeal request through the platform interface
- The appeal should include:
  - Clear explanation of why they believe the bounty was unfairly rejected
  - Any relevant evidence or documentation
  - Reference to the original bounty submission and rejection

### 2. Admin Review
- An admin team member reviews the appeal and the original bounty submission
- The admin evaluates:
  - The quality and completeness of the submitted work
  - Whether the work meets the specified requirements
  - Any potential misunderstandings or communication gaps
- The admin may:
  - Approve the bounty payment if they determine the work was valid
  - Request additional information from the contributor
  - Reject the appeal if the work doesn't meet the criteria

### 3. Community Vote (if applicable)
- For complex or contentious disputes, the platform may initiate a community vote
- Relevant community members are notified and given the opportunity to review the work
- The community votes on whether the bounty should be paid based on:
  - The quality of the work
  - Whether it meets the stated requirements
  - The overall benefit to the project
- The result of the community vote is binding

## Resolution Timeline
- Appeals should be submitted within 7 days of the original rejection
- Admin reviews typically take 3-5 business days
- Community votes, if initiated, take an additional 5-7 business days
- Final decisions are communicated to all parties involved

## Appeal Outcomes
- **Approved**: Bounty payment is authorized and processed
- **Rejected**: Original rejection decision stands
- **Additional Information Requested**: Contributor must provide more details before a final decision can be made
- **Community Vote Initiated**: Dispute is escalated to community review process

## Appealing a Decision
If a contributor is unhappy with the outcome of their appeal, they may:
1. Request a second review from a different admin
2. Provide additional evidence or clarification
3. Escalate to the project maintainers for final consideration

The goal of the dispute resolution process is to ensure fair treatment of all contributors while maintaining the quality standards of the project.
