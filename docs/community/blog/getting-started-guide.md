# Getting Started with SolFoundry: Your First Bounty

## Introduction

Welcome to SolFoundry! This guide will walk you through the process of finding and submitting your first bounty. Whether you're a developer looking to contribute to open-source projects or a Solana enthusiast wanting to support the ecosystem, this guide has you covered.

SolFoundry is a platform that connects developers with bounties for building and improving Solana-based projects. By participating in bounties, you can gain experience, contribute to meaningful projects, and earn rewards.

---

## Setting up Your Wallet

Before you can participate in bounties, you'll need a Solana-compatible wallet. Here's how to set one up:

### 1. Choose a Wallet Provider

Popular wallet options include:
- [Phantom](https://phantom.app/) (Browser extension)
- [Solflare](https://solflare.io/) (Desktop & Mobile)
- [Sollet](https://sollet.io/) (Browser extension)

### 2. Install and Configure Your Wallet

1. Download and install your chosen wallet extension or application
2. Create a new wallet and securely store your seed phrase
3. Fund your wallet with some SOL (you can get test SOL from [Solana Devnet Faucet](https://solfaucet.com/))

### 3. Connect to Solana Devnet

For testing purposes, connect to the Solana Devnet:
1. Open your wallet
2. Select "Devnet" as your network
3. Request test SOL from the faucet

## Finding a Bounty

Now that you're ready to contribute, let's find a bounty that interests you:

### 1. Browse Available Bounties

Visit the [SolFoundry Bounties Page](https://solfoundry.com/bounties) to explore available opportunities.

### 2. Filter by Your Interests

Use the search and filter options to find bounties that match:
- Your skill level (beginner, intermediate, advanced)
- Your preferred programming language
- The specific Solana ecosystem area you're interested in

### 3. Review Bounty Details

When you find a bounty that interests you:
- Read the description carefully
- Check the requirements and acceptance criteria
- Look at any provided examples or documentation
- Verify the bounty's status (open, in progress, closed)

---

## Submission Process

Once you've identified a bounty you want to work on, follow these steps to submit your solution:

### 1. Prepare Your Solution

1. **Understand the Requirements**: Make sure you fully understand what's being asked
2. **Develop Your Solution**: Build your implementation according to the bounty specifications
3. **Test Thoroughly**: Test your solution on both Devnet and local environments
4. **Document Your Work**: Include clear documentation explaining your implementation

### 2. Create a Pull Request

1. **Fork the Repository**: If the bounty is in a GitHub repository, fork it to your account
2. **Clone the Repository**: Clone your forked repository to your local machine
3. **Create a New Branch**: Create a branch for your bounty submission
4. **Commit Your Changes**: Add your implementation with clear commit messages
5. **Push to Your Fork**: Push your changes to your GitHub fork

### 3. Submit Your Bounty

1. **Visit the Bounty Page**: Go to the specific bounty page on SolFoundry
2. **Submit Your PR**: Enter the URL of your pull request
3. **Fill Out Submission Details**:
   - Provide a clear title for your submission
   - Write a detailed description of what you implemented
   - Include any relevant screenshots or demos
4. **Submit**: Click the submit button to send your bounty

### 4. Wait for Review

- The bounty maintainer will review your submission
- They may request changes or ask questions
- Be responsive to any feedback
- Once approved, you'll receive your reward!

---

## Tips for Success

1. **Start Small**: Begin with smaller bounties to build your confidence
2. **Communicate**: Don't hesitate to ask questions in the bounty's discussion forum
3. **Be Patient**: Review processes may take time
4. **Improve Continuously**: Use each bounty as a learning opportunity
5. **Give Back**: Consider submitting bounties of your own to help others
